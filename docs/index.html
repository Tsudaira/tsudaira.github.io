Driving the rise of Unicorn Startups

What makes a unicorn?

Grab, Lazada, and Carousell. What do these have in common?

Apart from being part of our everyday vocabulary, they are also some of Singaporeâ€™s most successful Unicorn Startups to date. Unicorns, not mythical creatures in folklore, but a rare breed of of startup companies that have taken the world by storm. In the realm of business, unicorns are startups that achieved an elusive status: a valuation of $1 billion or more, making them extremely sought-after entities.

Given the rarity of this phenomenon, it is expectedly tough for business owners to achieve such status. Within the company, you must be able to run a tight ship and operate on novel ideas, but externally, several factors could make or break your chances before you even set out. Here, we take a look at the environmental factors surrounding some of the most successful unicorns in the world and see how factors such as geographical placement and industry trends can help you better position your startup.

Rate of Unicorns by Country

Today, unicorns can be found globally, a stark contrast to a decade or two ago when the majority of them were founded in the United States. Looking at the number of startups by country, it is clear that the United States leads the pack in terms of the number of unicorns produced, followed by China, and India. This indicates a supportive startup ecosystem in such countries, where the bulk of startups are headquartered in cities such as California, Shanghai, and Bengaluru, where innovation and funding are much easier to come by. However, although these are certainly popular, such cities are not the only regions where startups can find success.

What is more intriguing is the rate of emergence of unicorns in countries such as China, South Korea, and Israel. Combined, the startups founded in the 3 countries are only a fraction of the number located in the United States, but boast much higher success rates of unicorns, with China sporting an unbelievable 1 in 4 startups turning into unicorns.

A reliable and supportive ecosystem is needed to find success for your business, but instead of only looking towards popular cities in the US or India, less competitive but viable options can be seen even in places such as Israel, Germany, and Singapore.

From the Valuation Map, it is also seen that the United States holds the highest total valuation ($2,052B) of unicorns. This indicates the strength of venture capital investments within the US itself. In second place is China, with $707.67B among 173 unicorns. Compared to India with $193.75B and 70 unicorns, the average valuation of unicorns in China is significantly higher. This might indicate a greater market potential for startups in China.

What do they do?

Another factor that would affect the success of startups is be industry they operate in. Based on the Industry treemaps, it is observed that most unicorns are found in Enterprise Tech, followed by Finance and Retail. The total valuation corresponds to the number of unicorns, highlighting the strong growth potential and investor confidence in these industries. On the other hand, Insurance sees the least number of startup unicorns, with the lowest valuation.

Recent Trends

Looking into industry trends, it can be seen that there has been a steady growth of unicorn startups since 2014. A significant spike in unicorns occurred during the height of Covid-19 in 2021 and 2022, likely fueled by a global push for digitalization and remote enterprise solutions. Since then, the numbers in 2023 have dropped back down, below that of 2020, likely a response to the increase in the US Fed Funds Interest Rates.

Looking closer at the valuation trends provided by the bubble chart, Enterprise and Finance have measured great successes in both volume and valuation over the past 5 years, whereas the remaining sectors appear to be slowing down, with fewer unicorns and lower valuations on average since 2022.

Conclusion

However, it should also be noted that countries such as France, Brazil, and Singapore, only sport just over 1000 startups but have a track record of over 1% turned unicorns could be potential sites for startups.

Industry-wise, Enterprise Tech, and Finance continue to show growth potential and are therefore the safest options for achieving unicorn status. However, given that many unicorns were made through some form of industry-disrupting service, current slow-moving industries may reveal higher chances of capturing market share.
